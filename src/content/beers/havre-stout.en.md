# Recipe (23 litres)

With an irresistibly smooth texture and full roasted chocolate flavours, the Oat
Stout is an exquisite, comforting beer to enjoy through the dark winter months.

## Malt

| Malt | Amount | Share |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pale ale malt | 4.2 kg | 87% |
| Rolled oats | 250 g | 5% |
| Caramel malt | 200 g | 4% |
| Chocolate malt | 160 g | 3% |
| Roasted barley | 70 g | 1% |

## Hops

- **Challenger** (7%) – 39 g at the start of the boil (30.3 IBU)
- **Challenger** (7%) – 16 g at flameout
- **Golding** (5.5%) – 16 g at flameout

## Other

- **Protofloc** – 1 tsp in the last 15 minutes

## Yeast

- Wyeast 1187 Ringwood Ale

## Mash

Mash in at **67 °C** with 12.2 litres and hold for 60 minutes. Boil 27 litres for
1 hour 10 minutes.

## Fermentation

Ferment at **20 °C**, then lager for 4 weeks at 12 °C. Ready to drink after about
5 weeks.

> Tip: The oats make the beer prone to oxidation, so avoid splashing and adding extra oxygen when bottling. 🍺
