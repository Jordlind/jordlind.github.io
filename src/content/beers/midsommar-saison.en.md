A classic Belgian saison brewed for midsummer. Dry, lightly peppery and with a
fruity yeast character. Perfect with seafood and late nights on the deck.

## Recipe (20 litres)

### Malt

| Malt | Amount | Share |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pilsner malt | 4.0 kg | 80% |
| Wheat malt | 0.5 kg | 10% |
| Munich malt | 0.5 kg | 10% |

### Hops

- **Saaz** – 25 g at 60 min
- **Saaz** – 15 g at 10 min
- **Styrian Goldings** – 15 g at flameout

### Yeast

- Wyeast 3724 Belgian Saison

### Mash

Mash in at **65 °C** and hold for 60 minutes. Raise to 72 °C for 15 minutes
before mashing out at 78 °C.

### Fermentation

Ferment warm! Start at 22 °C and let the temperature climb toward 28–30 °C during
the first week. Saison yeast loves heat and gets drier and more peppery the warmer
it works.

> Tip: Be patient — saison yeast can appear to stall but often restarts on its own.
